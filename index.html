What is trading?

Trading is the act of buying and selling assets—like stocks, currencies, cryptocurrencies, or goods—with the goal of making a profit from price changes.

In simple terms: you buy something at a lower price and try to sell it at a higher price (or sometimes the opposite, if you expect prices to fall).

In the quiz below you practice reading charts: what a highlighted candle often suggests when price is moving up or down. That helps you think about when entries and exits are more logical and when it is still too early to decide. Patterns are educational; on a live market always confirm with context and risk controls.

Learn first

Use a demo account, books, videos, and small-size tests before you commit larger capital.

Rules and discipline

Define when you enter, when you exit, and how much risk you accept per day or per trade.

Psychology

Greed and fear often move markets inside your head faster than indicators—plan for emotions.

Basics to know

  • Lot size / leverage — how much exposure you trade and how much margin you post.
  • Spread and commission — every trade has a cost; include it in your plan.
  • Stop-loss / take-profit — predefined exit levels to cap risk and lock gains.
  • Timeframe — a day trader works a different rhythm than a long-term investor.
  • Regulation — use a regulated broker where possible and read their terms.

Risk warning

Trading can lead to losing your entire capital. Leverage magnifies both gains and losses. This app is not financial advice and does not replace a professional adviser or your broker’s disclosures.

Practical steps to start

  1. 1

    Set a goal

    Learning, side income, or a career path—your goal shapes how much time and capital you need.

  2. 2

    Learn terms and the platform

    Start on demo, learn order types, and practice reading price and volume on charts.

  3. 3

    Keep a trading journal

    After each trade note why you entered and what happened—this speeds up real learning.

  4. 4

    Go live with small size

    When demo feels consistent, risk only money whose loss teaches you—not money you need for life.

Short glossary

Asset
What you buy or sell (e.g. a currency pair, stock, index).
Bullish / bearish
Expectation of prices moving up / down.
Volatility
How strongly price swings—higher volatility means higher risk and opportunity.
Liquidity
How easily you can trade size without moving price too much.
Diversification
Spreading risk across instruments or strategies.
